Shake vs. Stir Comparison
Shaking fractures ice, aerates, and emulsifies — creating texture and micro-bubbles that carry volatile aromatics. Stirring preserves clarity, controls dilution, and maintains surface tension. Neither is universally superior.

Ice Format Guide & Melt Rate
A 5 cm clear cube has 1/6th the surface area of the equivalent crushed ice. Less surface = slower melt = less dilution in glass. Directional freezing eliminates trapped air and mineral haze.

Supercooled Liquid & Freezing Point
Ethanol depresses water's freezing point. At 20% ABV, solution freezes at −8 °C. At 40% ABV, −16 °C. Supercooling below freezing without nucleation creates dramatic tableside crystallisation effects.
