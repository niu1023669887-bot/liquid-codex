Dehydration Tips ▾
  1. Slice uniformly — thickness variation causes uneven drying
  2. Blanch fruit 30s for brighter colour retention
  3. Pre-treat with lemon juice to prevent oxidation browning
  4. Rotate trays every 2–3 h for even airflow
  5. Cool to room temp before checking — test bend/snap
  6. Store in airtight container with silica gel sachet
Isomalt Sugar Art Temperature Guide
Isomalt: 1:1 D-glucitol + mannitol. Non-hygroscopic (resists moisture uptake). Pulled/blown at 155–160 °C; cast/poured at 165–175 °C; pulled tuiles and cages at 145–155 °C. Caramelises at 160 °C (slower than sucrose at 165 °C).

Fruit Leather / Pâte de Fruit Calculator
Fruit leather is dehydrated purée on a non-stick surface. For cocktail use, add lactic acid (1–2 g/L) for brightness and pectin (0.5–1%) for structure. Optimal spread thickness: 3–5 mm for flexible leathers; 8–10 mm for firm pâte de fruit.
